Why this exists

Why someone would build free tools for fire departments and what's NOT in scope.

The thinking

  • Volunteer fire is structurally underfunded for software. Big departments pay $50-200k/year for Mark43 / Tyler / Hexagon. Volunteer departments do it on Excel + Word + clipboard. AI is finally good enough to fill the gap for $0.
  • Admin work is the recruit-killer. Multiple studies — and every chief I've talked to — say the same thing: probationaries leave because of paperwork burden, not because of fire calls. Cutting paperwork keeps volunteers.
  • The technology is genuinely free. Modern LLMs have generous free tiers (Groq, Cerebras, Mistral, HuggingFace). Cloud Run scales to zero. A tool serving one department costs $0/month to operate.
  • I want to volunteer. I'm applying to my local department now. While that's processing, I can do something useful with my engineering skills that's not "send a check."

What's deliberately out of scope

  • Anything that touches dispatch decisions or scene tactics. AI hallucinations + life-safety = no. Hard line.
  • Anything that touches PHI / patient care. EMS run reports use NEMSIS, which is HIPAA-regulated. Not going there. NFIRS-1 is incident data, not patient data, which is why this app is OK.
  • Per-department customization that requires my time forever. The tools are designed to work for any department out of the box, with optional config. I can fork for a department, but I'm not running a managed service.
  • Replacing your CAD/RMS. These tools complement what you already have. Zero integration required.
