Why this exists

Why someone would build focused tools for fire departments and what's NOT in scope.

The thinking

  • Volunteer fire is structurally underfunded for software. Large departments can buy enterprise suites. Volunteer departments often rely on spreadsheets, documents, and clipboards. Focused AI can help close that gap without charging departments.
  • Administrative friction is worth measuring. Scheduling, training access, expectations, family support, and paperwork may affect recruiting or retention differently in each department. The tool treats those as hypotheses to test, not universal causes.
  • Access includes three previews, then Advanced service at $29.99/month. The tools use a restricted U.S. provider pool with automatic fallback, and Cloud Run scales to zero when idle. Departments do not need a paid plan, contract, or API key.
  • I want to volunteer. I'm applying to my local department now. While that's processing, I can do something useful with my engineering skills that's not "send a check."

What's deliberately out of scope

  • Anything that touches dispatch decisions or scene tactics. AI hallucinations + life-safety = no. Hard line.
  • Anything that touches PHI / patient care. Patient-care records can contain protected health information and require department-specific legal, security, and records controls. These tools do not accept patient narratives or replace NEMSIS/ePCR systems.
  • Per-department customization that requires my time forever. The tools are designed to work for any department out of the box, with optional config. I can fork for a department, but I'm not running a managed service.
  • Replacing your CAD/RMS. These tools complement what you already have. Zero integration required.
